PhillyFest

June 12 - June 14

Circus Campus

6452 Greene Street,

Philadelphia, PA 19119

The Philadelphia Juggling Festival is a full weekend of juggling, for professionals, avid hobbyists, and brand-new beginners! All are welcome at the “Learn to Juggle” station, and if you’d rather just watch the pros, the Saturday night Public Show will feature the best of the best. The Festival is hosted at Circus Campus, in the West Mt. Airy neighborhood of Philadelphia. Circus Campus features 20,000 sq ft of indoor training space: two large gymnasium spaces, smaller classrooms for workshops, and Greg Kennedy’s Innovative Juggler studio. There is also ample free parking and green outdoor space.

Show Lineup

Chris Hodge grew up juggling and performing with his father and brothers as the Hodge Family Jugglers. He attended l'Ecole de Cirque de Quebec. Now, he is a family doctor in upstate NY. Jonah and Chris set up a gravitational forces lab together a year ago. The primary outcome of their research thus far has been the world record for juggling the most rings between 2 people.

Lauren Raske is a performer specializing in fire, light, and illusionary arts. As founder and creative director of the specialty artists agency, 7textures, she advocates for and manages a wide network of artistic talent across many disciplines and mediums. Her passion project is crafting site-specific stunt theater experiences like the Ghostly Circus, a 501c3 project of CultureTrust Philadelphia. Photo Credit: RD Buugeng

Alli Hart - aka The Hoop Doc - is a multidisciplinary hula hoop and circus artist based in Philadelphia. She is also a Doctor of Physical Therapy who specializes in treating performing artists including dancers and circus artists. She discovered her love for hula hoops and circus while studying dance at Drexel University. Coming from an extensive performing arts and gymnastics background, Alli has been hula hooping for 12 years and juggling hoops for 8 years.

Jonah Botvinick-Greenhouse specializes in dynamical systems, mathematical physics & inverse problems at Cornell's Center for Applied Mathematics. He also throws and catches things.

Tim Air-Walker is a self-taught Club Juggler and Content Creator based out of North Texas. He started juggling clubs in 2015 and almost instantly fell in love with Circular Aesthetics, Flat Throws, & Curvy Patterns within club juggling.
